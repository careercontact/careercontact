Speakers Summary:

  • Varun Mittal, Ernst & Young (EY) Partner, EY Global Emerging Markets FinTech Leader: As EY Global Emerging Markets FinTech Leader, Varun is responsible for EY FinTech business development in 130 emerging markets across the globe. Varun has deep experience in the FinTech domain and is skilled in cross-border management of FinTech operations and business planning, digital banking innovation and strategy, and regulatory strategy development (M&A). His specialization also covers FinTech ecosystem development, FinTech strategy development and implementation, FinTech corporate finance due diligence, and solution development and intrapreneurship. Varun holds a Master of Strategy and Operations from National University of Singapore, and a Bachelor of Technology (Computer Science Engineering) from Jaypee Institute of Information Technology, India. He is a founding member of the Singapore FinTech Association, the only non-profit FinTech body supported by the Monetary Authority of Singapore.

Questions asked and answered

Chat Summary

  • What is your career journey?

    Varun started out as a computer science engineer and did programming. He then completed an MBA at NUS and founded his own startup before joining EY.

  • What advice do you have to give students?

    It is important to explore widely—try as many internships as possible to find out what you really enjoy before choosing a specific career path. “Try all the food in the buffet before deciding which restaurant you want long-term membership in.”

  • What do you have to share about FinTech?
    Fintech is extremely prevalent in our daily lives. Some examples include Grab, Gojek, payWave, smart watches, microsavings. GrabPay allows credit line pay in three installments without interest. Things are getting increasingly digital, for example, digitalising what one does to book a vacation. There are many young investors and a lot of Fintech start-ups in Singapore. FinTech is not just limited to start-ups, companies have FinTech departments too. ByteDance applied for a banking license in Singapore; XiaoMi also owns banks. There are a range of careers in the FinTech industry, including those that go beyond finance and technology, such as marketing. Cryptocurrency normalising is unlikely in the next five years.
  • What are you most interested in with regards to FinTech?
    He is interested in all aspects, and what excites him is the product.
  • What challenges do you face in your job?
    There are different challenges such as the target group.
  • What skills are needed in Fintech?
    What is most important is understanding consumer behaviour and being able to think through the consumer experience. Different kinds of marketing also require user research.
  • Coding vs engineering?
    Coding is programming while engineering is designing.
